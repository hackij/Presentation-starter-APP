Chapter 3

Presentation Opening Techniques

Strong speakers rarely begin with weak biography lines such as "Hello my name is...". Instead, they create attention immediately and then lead the audience into the topic.

IMG
Image

Show a striking visual that immediately raises curiosity.

?
Question

Ask something that makes the audience think before you explain.

QT
Quote

Use a short powerful statement to frame your topic.

ST
Anecdote

Tell a short story that makes the topic personal and memorable.

!
Surprising Fact

Start with an unexpected detail that challenges assumptions.

Chapter 4

Case Study: Steve Jobs Introducing the iPhone in 2007

Steve Jobs did not simply announce a product. He created suspense, built a rhythm and made the audience feel that they were about to witness something historic.

He built suspense.

Jobs delayed the key reveal and let the audience anticipate what was coming.

He activated the audience.

He used pauses, repetition and emphasis so listeners were mentally involved instead of passive.

The opening worked because it felt like an event.

It combined clarity, confidence and surprise in a way that made the audience eager for the next sentence.

Chapter 5

Body Language & Voice

Your opening is not only about words. The audience also reads your posture, eye contact, pauses and vocal energy in the first seconds.

Body Language Visual

Recommended posture: welcoming

Good presenters should appear open, confident and welcoming.

Avoid closed or defensive body language.

1
Posture

Stand tall and balanced. It signals control and readiness.

2
Eye contact

Connect with people across the room instead of looking down.

3
Pauses

Use silence to let an idea land and build anticipation.

4
Voice projection

Speak clearly enough to reach the back of the room.

5
Confidence

Move slowly, breathe deeply and avoid rushed speech.

Key Presentation Tips

Open with stillness
Look up before speaking
Use your first pause deliberately
Let confidence show in your pace
Project, do not shout
